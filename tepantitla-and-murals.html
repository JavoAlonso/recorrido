This name refers to a "palace" type housing complex where the inhabitants were either member of the priestly caste or of a leading family in Teotihuacan's society.

 

Other sections of the group are readily reached from this central courtyard. It is in them where visitors can enjoy some outstanding murals with varying scenes. Near the southeast side, you find the "Red Tlaloc" mural, so named because the painting, executed in hues of red, is centered around this water god. Another mural is "El Tlalocan", meaning Tlaloc's paradise. In the next room is a procession of priests attired in splendid vestments and plumed headdresses. Toward the north of the courtyard are paintings of round shields, while on the south side there are surviving remains of other murals in which also appears a priestly procession.
